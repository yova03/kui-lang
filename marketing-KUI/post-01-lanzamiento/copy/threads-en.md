**1/4**

I spent more time fighting LaTeX than writing my thesis.

So I built my own document language. It's called KUI. A single line of text becomes a numbered figure in the final PDF. No packages, no distributions, no external compiler.

---

**2/4**

The rule was simple: if I have to open Stack Overflow to insert an image, the format is broken.

In KUI:

`imagen kui-logo | KUI logotype`

That's it. Auto caption, auto numbering.

---

**3/4**

KUI was born writing an actual archaeology dissertation at UNSAAC, in Cusco, Peru. Modular chapters, bibliography, TOC, numbered figures — all from a single main file.

Every design choice was validated against a real academic case, not a demo.

---

**4/4**

It's open source. Validator, templates, native PDF renderer, and an optional `.tex` exporter for those who need to interop.

If LaTeX has hurt you, or if you're writing your thesis next to an AI assistant, this might be worth a look.

https://github.com/yova03/kui-lang
